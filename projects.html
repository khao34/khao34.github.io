2015-2016

Air Trajectory

Objective: Teams will design, construct, and calibrate a device capable of launching a projectile into a target area and collect data to develop a series of graphs relate to the capabilities of the device.
Operation: Mass free-falls a certain height before compressing the air in the PVC chamber launching the ping pong ball.
Awards:

  • Massachusetts Institute of Technology Invitational Tournament: 5th place

  • Florida Regional Tournament: 4th place

  • Florida State Tournament: 6th place

Electric Vehicle

Objective: Teams must design, build and test one vehicle that uses electrical energy as its sole means of propulsion to travel as quickly as possible and stop close to a Target Point.
Operation: Three Arduino microprocessors used to read a rotary encoder, control the motor, and accept user input. Together, the system interconnected via I2C accurately travels the given distance with millimeter precision.
Awards:

  • Florida State Tournament: 1st place

  • Science Olympiad National Tournament: 2nd place

Robot Arm

Objective: Prior to the competition, teams must design, build, document and test one robotic device to move scoreable items.
Operation: Three Arduino microcontrollers are used to create a master-slave closed feedback loop between the potentiometers in the controllers and the servos in the arm.
Awards:

  • Cypress Falls Invitational Tournament: 1st place
